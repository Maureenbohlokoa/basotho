Traditional Dances & Instruments



Traditional dances

Mohobelo Dance

A traditional male dance performed with stomping movements, symbolizing unity and strength.

Setapo Dance

A vibrant dance performed by women, showcasing elegance, grace, and storytelling through movement.

Mokhibo

A womenâ€™s dance where they kneel, clap, and move their shoulders while singing. It shows elegance and group harmony.

Litolobonya

A playful dance for young girls where they move in light steps and sing together. It helps pass on cultural values.

Tsoalela

A celebratory dance done at weddings and gatherings. It has energetic moves and happy songs to bring people together.

Seakhi

A cultural dance performed during harvest festivals. It thanks ancestors for blessings and good crops.

Mokorotlo

A warlike dance linked to the famous Basotho hat. It uses stamping and chanting to show bravery and identity.

Seahlolo

A group dance where men and women answer each other through song and movement, showing unity.

Leeba

A fast-paced youth dance with quick stamping and clapping. It shows excitement and energy.



Traditional instruments

Lesiba Instrument

A wind instrument made from a quill and string, known for producing unique resonant sounds.

Thomo

A mouth-bow instrument usually played by women. It makes soft, calming melodies often used in storytelling.

Sekhankula

A one-string instrument played with a stick, often by herd boys. It creates rhythmic sounds while tending animals.

Moropa

A drum that sets the rhythm for dances and ceremonies. It brings power and unity to performances.

Lekope

A drum that sets the rhythm for dances and ceremonies. It brings power and unity to performances.

Moropa oa letlalo

A hide-covered drum used in rituals and festivals. It produces deep, echoing beats that guide dancing.

Phala ea kholumo

A large horn often blown at important traditional events. Its sound carries far and marks special moments.

Likhoka

Bells worn on the legs of dancers. They jingle as the dancer moves, adding sound to the rhythm.

Seqha

A bow-like instrument sometimes used for music and hunting. It can make twanging sounds for entertainment.

Linaka

Wooden or reed pipes played together in harmony. They create melodies for dances and community celebrations.

Phala

A horn made from an animal horn. It is blown in ceremonies or to call people together.

Lekhori

A traditional rattle made from seeds or shells. It adds rhythm and excitement to dances

Mamokhorong

A violin-like instrument made with a tin can, string, and bow. It plays simple, traditional tunes.
